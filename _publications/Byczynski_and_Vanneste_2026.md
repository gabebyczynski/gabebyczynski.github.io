We discuss evoked brainstem responses including brainstem evoked audiometry, which additionally provide valuable information as a direct representation of brainstem activation and pathology. Lastly, we discuss the application of brainstem responses and nerve stimulation in a surgical setting via intraoperative recording. We conclude by discussing the value of these measures and methods in brainstem research.
